GARCH and MA Outperformance

Now Using d3 Parallel Coordinates

Parallel coordinates become much more useful when they are interactive, so I will recreate one of my favorite blog posts "Trend is Not Your Friend" Applied to 48 Industries and convert the chart to a living breathing d3 parallel coordinates chart courtesy of rCharts and Kai Chang's d3.parcoords.

Setup

require(rCharts)

cars <- read.csv("data/cars.csv")
p1 <- rCharts$new()
p1$field("lib", "libraries/widgets/parcoords")
p1$set(padding = list(top = 24, left = 100, bottom = 12, right = 200))
p1$set(data = toJSONArray(cars, json = F), colorby = "economy", range = range(cars$economy), 
    colors = c("steelblue", "brown"))
p1
p1$print("chart")
